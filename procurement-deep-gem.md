Procurement Module — Deep DiveA comprehensive architectural specification for the AMSRef Procurement Module, detailing end-to-end lifecycle management across four procurement categories, strict adherence to NHS Standing Financial Instructions (SFIs), integration with the Procurement Act 2023, and deep interoperability with core asset, job, contract, and stock management modules.1. OverviewThe AMSRef Procurement Module is conceptualized as an independently deployable, event-driven orchestration engine. It acts as the critical bridge connecting clinical engineering needs, financial controls, supply chain logistics, and regulatory compliance frameworks. Operating within a multi-tenant architecture secured by strict data scoping (Footprints), the module ensures that a hospital department managing tens of thousands of medical devices can execute acquisitions ranging from simple consumables to multi-million-pound capital projects without circumventing NHS governance protocols.The architecture relies heavily on cross-module event emitters. State transitions within the procurement lifecycle trigger automated cascades in downstream modules—such as auto-generating Commission Requests in the Asset Lifecycle module, incrementing stock_level in the Spare Parts module, or instantiating a MAINTENANCE_CONTRACT. This interconnected design eliminates redundant data entry and maintains an immutable thread of data provenance from initial requisition through to asset decommissioning.1.1 Master Procurement Lifecycle DiagramThe following master workflow illustrates how the unified procurement request diverges based on the evaluated category and converges into operational AMSRef entities.Code snippetflowchart TD

Budget & Financial IntegrationThe Procurement Module does not passively record spending; it actively prevents budget breaches through sophisticated financial controls.8.1 Commitment and Encumbrance TrackingNHS finance dictates that open commitments must be factored into projected expenses to determine true solvency.Pre-Encumbrance: When a Requisition_Draft is pushed to Local_Approval, the system queries the target BUDGET. If the mathematical equation (total_budget - actual_spend - existing_encumbrances) < request_value evaluates to true, the submission is blocked due to insufficient funds. If false, a PRE_ENCUMBRANCE record is generated.Encumbrance: The moment an EQUIPMENT_ORDER is dispatched, the pre-encumbrance converts to a legally binding ENCUMBRANCE, reserving the funds.Actual Expenditure: When the GRN is logged and the invoice matched, the encumbrance is relieved and logged as an ACTUAL expenditure.8.2 VAT Determination AlgorithmsVAT compliance represents a significant risk vector. The system leverages metadata to algorithmically flag appropriate tax treatments:Charity Zero-Rating: If a capital bid specifies a hospital charitable fund as the budget_id, the system checks if the MODEL.category maps to HMRC's definition of "relevant medical equipment" (designed specifically for diagnosis or treatment). If true, the tax_treatment_flag is set to ZERO_RATED_CHARITY, fulfilling HMRC Notice 701/6 requirements.Contracted Out Services (COS): For Category C service contracts encompassing managed equipment services, the system evaluates if the contract meets COS Heading 14 criteria. If the contract provides sufficient 'labor and expertise' rather than simple equipment hire, the system flags the VAT as recoverable, optimizing NHS trust budgets.8.3 Auto-Generation of Depreciation SchedulesUpon the successful generation of a Commission Request via the Category A workflow, the financial engine evaluates the MODEL.category and applies standard NHS accounting depreciation rules on a straight-line basis.Standard plant and machinery are mapped to a 5-10 year lifecycle.High-risk, specialized medical devices (e.g., ventilators, defibrillators, heart-lung machines) are automatically coded for a 13-16 year replacement lifecycle. These fields populate the asset's Depreciation Period and Life (years) fields via the auto-fill cascade.9. NHS Regulatory ComplianceThe module operates as a compliance firewall, ensuring end-users cannot circumvent statutory laws or patient safety directives.9.1 Procurement Act 2023 EnforcementPost-Brexit, the Procurement Act 2023 replaces the Public Contracts Regulations (PCR) 2015 and the OJEU process. The system is built around the new "Competitive Flexible Procedure".When a Category A or D procurement exceeds the PCR threshold (£214,904), the module enforces the mandatory notice sequence via API integration with the Find a Tender service :UK1 Pipeline Notice: Auto-published for major upcoming capital projects.UK2 Preliminary Market Engagement Notice: Logged to document early supplier involvement.UK4 Tender Notice: Published to formally invite bids. The state machine hard-locks the Tender_Active state, enforcing mandatory minimum time periods (e.g., 25 days for electronic submission, 30 days if documents are not provided simultaneously).UK6 & UK7 Contract Notices: Contract Award Notice (UK6) and Contract Details Notice (UK7) are auto-published upon FBC approval and final execution.9.2 Medical Device Safety & DTAC 2026 IntegrationThe clinical safety of an asset must be validated before a Purchase Order is generated. The EVALUATION_MATRIX entity acts as the gatekeeper.UK MDR 2002 Compliance: The system validates supplier compliance with the Medical Devices Regulations 2002. It checks the manufacturer's UKCA marking status or explicitly recognizes acceptable international equivalencies (CE mark, FDA 510k) as defined by MHRA transition rules.Digital Technology Assessment Criteria (DTAC): If the MODEL is flagged as Software as a Medical Device (SaMD), the system mandates a DTAC assessment. Aligning with the February 2026 DTAC framework, the supplier portal ingest enforces the 25% streamlined questionnaire, de-duplicating requirements already covered by the DSPT and PAQ. The supplier must prove Cyber Essentials certification, FHIR API interoperability, and WCAG 2.1 accessibility. A failing DTAC score permanently disables the FBC approval transition.10. Role-Based Access ControlThe Authorization model leverages the existing 3-layer system to strictly partition access, ensuring clinical staff cannot manipulate financial approvals and vice versa.13.1 Groups and Feature FlagsBiomedical Engineer: Granted CRUD capabilities on their own PROCUREMENT_REQUESTs (Draft stage only). They have read-only access to line items but are explicitly denied access to the Financials and Approvals tabs.Service Manager: Requires the new CAN_APPROVE_PROCUREMENT feature flag. Can transition requests up to the £50,000 threshold, strictly limited within their designated Service Footprint.Procurement Manager (New Role): Possesses global read access. Requires CAN_PUBLISH_TENDER and CAN_CREATE_PO feature flags. This is the only group with the authority to override DTAC/PAQ warnings (all overrides are immutably logged for audit).Budget Holder (New Role): Requires the CAN_OVERRIDE_ENCUMBRANCE feature flag. Can view all requests currently hitting their designated budget_id, regardless of the originating clinical department.13.2 Data Scoping (Footprints)Given the extreme sensitivity of financial and tender data, a WHERE clause footprint automatically appends to all user queries: WHERE pr.site_id IN (User.Sites) AND pr.service_id IN (User.Services). Key Business Rules SummaryThe following immutable constraints dictate the behavior of the Procurement Module and cannot be bypassed without database-level administrative intervention:Strict Segregation of Duties: A user initiating a PROCUREMENT_REQUEST is systematically barred from executing the approval state transition for that specific request, unconditionally overriding any RBAC permissions or threshold authority they may possess.Mathematical Encumbrance Enforcement: The system will abort any transition from Draft to Local_Approval if the evaluated equation (total_budget - actual_spend - existing_encumbrances) < request_value resolves to true.Procurement Act 2023 Mandatory Sequencing: Any Category A or D request exceeding the statutory PCR threshold (e.g., £214,904) automatically disables the "Direct Award" pathway, algorithmically enforcing the generation of a TENDER record and blocking PO generation until UK4 Tender Notices have passed their 25-day minimum window.Clinical Safety Gateways: A Category A PROCUREMENT_REQUEST cannot progress to the FBC Approval state or PO generation unless the selected MODEL possesses an 'APPROVED' Pre-Acquisition Questionnaire (PAQ) and, if flagged as SaMD, a compliant DTAC score.Asset Instantiation Cascade: Processing a Goods Received Note (GRN) for capital equipment automatically instantiates a Commission Request in the Asset Lifecycle module. This request permanently inherits the PO's financial data (purchase_price, derived warranty parameters) and is irreversibly linked to the original budget_id.VAT Notice 701/6 Verification: The Financial Engine will only permit a zero-rated VAT calculation if the funding source is verified as a registered Charitable Fund AND the selected MODEL.category explicitly maps to HMRC's definition of "Relevant Medical Equipment."Emergency Bypass Ratification: Invoking a 'State of Urgency' permits immediate PO dispatch to prevent clinical harm, but locks the transaction in an asynchronous compliance queue.
